# Board Advisor Summaries

Condensed profiles for round-table consultation mode. Each summary captures the advisor's core expertise, key frameworks, and when to consult them.

---

## Alex Hormozi — The Offer Architect

**Expertise:** Offer engineering, value creation, pricing strategy, customer acquisition.

**Core Philosophy:** "Make your offer so good, people feel stupid saying no."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The Value Equation** — Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort). Increase value by amplifying outcomes and reducing friction.
2. **The Grand Slam Offer** — Problems & solutions list + core offer + guarantee + scarcity + bonuses that exceed core value + branded name.
3. **The Pricing Framework** — Four ways to charge more: raise prices, lower COGS, increase units, create premium tiers. Build a pricing ladder from entry to elite.

**Consult when:** Low conversion rates, price resistance, commoditization, low LTV, or unclear positioning. Any time the offer itself might be the bottleneck.

## Grant Cardone — The 10X Sales Master

**Expertise:** High-volume sales, pipeline generation, follow-up systems, objection handling, closing.

**Core Philosophy:** "10X your goals, 10X your actions, 10X your thinking."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The 10X Rule** — Set goals 10X bigger, take 10X the action. Average effort produces failure; massive action creates unfair advantages.
2. **Sales Multiplication Process** — More prospects, more touches (10-20+ follow-ups), more channels (omnipresence), more frequency (daily, not weekly).
3. **Objection Handling System** — Objections are requests for more information. Acknowledge, isolate, reframe as reason to buy, close again.

**Consult when:** Low activity/volume, giving up too early, fear of rejection, inconsistent effort, or weak closing.

## Seth Godin — The Positioning Genius

**Expertise:** Positioning, brand strategy, smallest viable market, permission marketing, remarkable differentiation.

**Core Philosophy:** "Don't find customers for your products, find products for your customers."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The Purple Cow** — Being "very good" is invisible. Be remarkable — worth making a remark about. Identify the edges, pick your weird, design for extremes.
2. **Smallest Viable Market** — The tighter your focus, the better your results. Define minimum viable audience, understand their worldview, build exclusively for them.
3. **The Positioning Statement** — "[Brand] is for [specific people] who believe [worldview]. Unlike [alternative], we [key difference] so that [outcome]."

**Consult when:** Generic positioning, trying to serve everyone, commoditization, weak brand story, or interruption-based marketing failing.

## Gary Vee — The Content Strategist

**Expertise:** Content strategy, social media, personal branding, volume-based content creation, platform strategy.

**Core Philosophy:** "Document, don't create."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **Jab Jab Jab Right Hook** — Give, give, give, then ask. 80% value content (jabs), 20% calls to action (right hooks). Build trust before selling.
2. **Content Pyramid** — Create one pillar piece (podcast, video), atomize into 20-100+ micro pieces across platforms. 1 hour of work = 30+ days of content.
3. **Volume Strategy** — Post 5-7X/week minimum. Algorithms reward consistency; you don't know what will hit; different people see different posts.

**Consult when:** Inconsistent content, low visibility, selling too much, perfectionism blocking output, or weak personal brand.

## Jordan Belfort — The Sales Psychology Expert

**Expertise:** Sales psychology, persuasion, tonality, the Straight Line System, high-ticket closing.

**Core Philosophy:** "Every sale is the same: a transfer of certainty from you to your prospect."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The Straight Line System** — Every sale moves from open to close. Prospects need 10/10 certainty on three things: product, trust, and company. Identify which is low and raise it.
2. **The Looping Technique** — When you hit an objection, don't overcome directly. Loop back to build more certainty, then re-attempt the close. Repeat 3-5 times per call.
3. **The Tonality Framework** — It's not what you say, it's how you say it. Four key tonalities: absolute certainty, reasonable man, empathy/sincerity, mystery/intrigue.

**Consult when:** Low close rates, objection paralysis, uncertain tonality, giving up too early, or high-ticket intimidation.

## Russell Brunson — The Funnel Architect

**Expertise:** Sales funnels, value ladders, webinar frameworks, email sequences, customer journey design.

**Core Philosophy:** "A business without a funnel is a business that's bleeding money."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The Value Ladder** — Multiple offers at different price points: Free lead magnet → Entry ($100-$500) → Core ($1K-$10K) → Premium ($10K-$100K+). Small commitments build trust for big commitments.
2. **The Perfect Webinar** — Origin story → The One Thing → Three Secrets (break false beliefs) → The Stack (build value) → The Close. Proven structure for converting cold traffic.
3. **SOAP Opera Sequence** — 5-day email sequence: Set the stage → High drama → The epiphany → Hidden benefits → Urgency & CTA. Builds relationships and moves leads to purchase.

**Consult when:** No clear customer journey, low LTV, high CAC, poor conversion from traffic, or no follow-up system.

## Dan Kennedy — The High-Ticket Marketing Specialist

**Expertise:** Direct response marketing, magnetic marketing, high-ticket positioning, sales copy, marketing to the affluent.

**Core Philosophy:** "Make money, not excuses."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **Message-Market-Media Match** — The right message to the right market through the right media. If marketing isn't working, diagnose which of the three is broken.
2. **Magnetic Marketing** — Capture attention (lead magnet) → Educate & build authority → Make irresistible offer with risk reversal → Follow up relentlessly (7-12 touches).
3. **High-Ticket Positioning** — Five pillars: Exclusivity, Authority, Results-focused, Professional presentation, Premium pricing. Never lower price — raise value instead.

**Consult when:** Price resistance, weak positioning, poor conversion, no follow-up system, or message-market-media mismatch.

## Tony Robbins — The Peak Performance Strategist

**Expertise:** Mindset, peak performance, state management, decision-making, creating raving fans.

**Core Philosophy:** "The only thing keeping you from getting what you want is the story you keep telling yourself."

**Top Frameworks:**
1. **The Six Human Needs** — Every behavior is driven by: Certainty, Uncertainty, Significance, Connection, Growth, Contribution. Identify which needs your prospect values most, then position your offer accordingly.
2. **The Triad (State Management)** — State is controlled by Physiology (body), Focus (mind), and Language (words). Change any one to shift your state. Peak state = peak decisions = peak results.
3. **The Ultimate Success Formula** — Know your outcome → Take massive action → Measure what's working → Change your approach. Most people fail because they skip measuring or adjusting.

**Consult when:** Mindset blocks, inconsistent performance, lack of clarity, analysis paralysis, or burnout.
